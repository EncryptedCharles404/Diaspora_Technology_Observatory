The Process

From Patent Database to Your Factory Floor

We do the research so your engineers and procurement teams don't have to.

Common Questions
Is the patent library really free, permanently?
Yes. The core library — browsing all patents, viewing details, and requesting one free report — will always be free. The mission of DTO is access to knowledge. Paid tiers add convenience and depth, not gatekeeping.
When will Pro launch?
Pro is currently in development. The exact launch date depends on demand from the waitlist and the time to build the infrastructure properly. Joining the waitlist is the best way to get an early-access slot and discounted pricing.
What is a Technology Access Report?
It is a tailored PDF document listing expired patents and free technology access routes specific to your sector and country. It includes implementation notes, WIPO programme links, regulatory pathways, and saving potential. The first one is free for every enquiry via the Request Report page.
Can African universities or government agencies get access?
Yes — the Institutional tier is specifically designed for organisations like universities, ministry departments, development finance institutions, and innovation hubs. Contact us via the Request Report page to discuss your organisation's needs. Pricing is flexible for public-sector and academic bodies.
Is this legal? Can I actually use these patents?
Yes. Expired patents are in the public domain — anyone can use the technology without permission or payment. Patent Pool technologies require a free sublicence from the Medicines Patent Pool or similar body. We provide the implementation notes and regulatory guidance for each entry. You should always consult your national regulatory authority before manufacturing.
