## What This Is

The **Diaspora Technology Observatory (DTO)** is a research and intelligence platform that maps industrial technologies available to African businesses at **zero licensing cost** — through expired patents, Medicines Patent Pool sublicences, WIPO programmes, and international development frameworks that most African manufacturers don't know exist.

This repository hosts the full DTO Patent Intelligence Portal — a live, searchable database of **80 verified public domain patents and free technology access routes** for manufacturers across all 54 AfCFTA member states.

## What the Portal Does

- 🔍 **Searchable patent library** — 80 verified entries, filterable by sector, access type, and keyword
- 🌍 **Pan-African scope** — all entries verified for applicability across AfCFTA member states
- ✅ **Three access types tracked** — Public Domain, Patent Pool (Medicines Patent Pool), and Compulsory License (TRIPS Article 31)
- 📋 **Implementation notes per entry** — regulatory pathways (NAFDAC, SAHPRA, KEBS etc.), WIPO programme links, legal frameworks
- 📩 **Report request form** — visitors submit their sector and country and receive a tailored Technology Access Report within 48 hours, free of charge
- 💰 **Pricing page** — Free, Pro (coming soon), and Institutional tiers clearly outlined
- 📬 **Waitlist capture** — email signups for Pro tier early access

## Technology

Built as a **single-file HTML application** — no frameworks, no build tools, no server required. Fully deployable on Netlify, GitHub Pages, or any static hosting platform.

**Stack:**
- Vanilla HTML, CSS, JavaScript
- Google Fonts (Playfair Display, DM Sans, DM Mono)
- Formspree (contact form and waitlist submissions)
- Brevo (email list management)

**To add a new patent entry:** Edit the `DB` array in `index.html` and commit. No admin panel or build step required.

## The Opportunity

Africa imported **$542 billion in manufactured goods in 2023** — much of the underlying technology is already free. When a patent expires after 20 years, any company anywhere in the world can use that technology at zero licensing cost.

Most African manufacturers do not know this exists.

The DTO finds it, verifies it, and shows manufacturers exactly how to access it — legally, safely, and for free.
